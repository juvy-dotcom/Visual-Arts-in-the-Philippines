Quotes of Famous Philippine Visual Artists

Benedicto Cabrera
“BenCab”

National Artist for Visual Arts – Painter (2006)


“We always have a kind of elusive Filipino soul. I try to catch our nuances as Filipinos in my work.”


Source: Esquire Philippines: Benedicto Cabrera, Filipino Artist in Flux (2024)

Arturo Luz

National Artist for Visual Arts – Painter (1997)


“In my own little world, I know precisely what I want to do. And for me, the greatest satisfaction comes from creating works of art. Nothing can compare to it”

Source: BusinessWorld Online: Arturo Luz’s minimalist works of art

Napoleon Abueva

National Artist for Visual Arts – Sculpture (1976)


“Whatever desire is expressed in form, one conceives in realm of design. The mind in the hand can falter or surpass: if you answer which one and how much was done by love.”

Source: An Interview with National Artist for Sculpture Napoleon Abueva

Ang Kiukok

National Artist for Visual Arts (2001) Cubist, Surrealist, and Expressionist


During the Marcos regime, the National Artist has painted more brutal photographs; when asked why he was always filled with so much anger, he replied: "Why not? Open your eyes. Look at you. So much rage, sadness, ugliness, and insanity, too."

Source: Tatler: 5 Things You May Not Know About Ang Kiukok

José Joya

National Artist for Visual Arts (2003) Pioneer of Abstract Expressionism in the Philippines

“In my works I always celebrate the women of the Philippines. I regard them with deep admiration and they continue to inspire me … I know very well the strength, hard work, and quiet dignity of Philippine women, for I am one of them.

Source: Geringer Art: Biography of José Joya
